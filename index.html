Ms. Megha Parmar is a passionate mountaineer and technical diver hailing from Sehore district, Madhya Pradesh. She has earned several prestigious accolades, displaying her tryst with mountaineering.

She became the first woman from Madhya Pradesh to climb Mt. Everest on May 22nd, 2019. She has also scaled highest mountains of four other continents.
Ms. Megha has also set a new world record for technical scuba diving by diving to a depth of 147 feet (45 meters) in 27 minutes,at Bay of Bengal, Puducherry and became the first women in the world to achieve it.

Ms. Megha is an inspiring and a reputed individual. She has been facilitated and invited by various esteemed institutions and organisations, viz. University of Delhi, and different state government programs as a motivational speaker and acknowledging her contribution towards the society especially towards women and transgender.
She also appeared on talk shows like DD Madhya Pradesh and other digital platforms like ‘Techtalks’ and ‘Josh Talk’ as well.

Since 2019, she is Madhya Pradesh’s Brand Ambassador for the ‘BETI BACHAO BETI PADHAO’ campaign.

WHERE THERE’S A WILL, THERE'S A WAY
JOURNEY TO TOP

Each one carries a thought to do something big in their life, I had that feeling missing in my life for a very long. But, life had something very unique to offer and it all commenced with a simple article in the newspaper stating that 2 boys from Madhya Pradesh have accomplished their goals to climb Mt. Everest. It gave me a different perspective towards my upcoming life as being an athletic and adventurous human, the article somehow gave wings to my thoughts and excited me in and out completely. I had an agenda clear in my eyes to be the first woman to climb Mt. Everest from the very same state and through this, my dream took a fly and I started to prepare for it completely.

22 MAY 2019, THE DAY I HOSTED OUR FLAG ON
THE TOP OF MT. EVEREST AND MADE IT OCCUR

Happily, I received 15 lakh sponsorship from Madhya Pradesh Government and even some other sponsors and helped me reach the desired amount.

We had to complete our summit on 21 May but due to bad weather condition we had to wait for another day, again I got disheartened and couldn’t sleep all night thinking about it and many question marks were in my head, even the second challenge was limited oxygen cylinder, I had to decide whether I have to go or not, so after thinking I decided to go on 22 May 2019 and at 5:00 am of the dawn with the stroke of the light I successfully hosted the Indian flag at the top of Mt Everest with various other people from different countries with high pride and happiness. It became the proudest moment of my life and everything so like the perfect moment but my happiness faded soon after that as my oxygen cylinder was running out of oxygen.

8 AUGUST 2019, FIRST WOMAN OF MP TO CLIMB
MOUNT ELBRUS ( HIGHEST IN RUSSIA )

The journey which I started had no boundaries, it is ever going and by carrying a lot of enthusiasm and focus towards doing for what I was meant to do, another round of success came up to me when I became the first woman from Madhya Pradesh to climb Mount Elbrus in Russia.

It was all the worth of my training and hard work that I put throughout the sessions. It was like traveling near my dreams at a rapid speed. My self esteem was developing and the faith which I had and my father had in was resulting in a positive aspect.

With this, I decided to climb all the peaks of the seven continents and make space for my name in history for being the First woman from Madhya Pradesh by making my family and my state proud.

3 APRIL 2018, - MY ARRIVAL AT MT. EVEREST

My journey to Mt. Everest started with a challenge as my rigorous physical training and the strength-building process were upmost and I was ready to be at heights. We initiated our journey with full positivity but the day we were about to be on the top of Mt. Everest, the weather conditions, as well as our physical limitations, were turning against our will and sadly we had to return just from a distance of 700m to the Everest and every desire was shut down in seconds. All the efforts, training, and beliefs were like breaking down into tiny pieces, and couldn’t imagine seeing my destination in front but couldn’t manage to touch it and feel that very moment.

I fought a lot with my depression and constant taunts from people which made me a strong person and cycle again started I started my training again with the same passion and determination but this time during the course of my training session we were rock climbing and one of my teammate lost the grip of the safety rope and I fall from 30ft height straight on my back which resulted in 3 hairline fractures in my spinal cord, doctors suggested to quit my dreams and was recommended bed rest for 8 months, everyone with and around me that it’s over the dream I had no destination now but the believe I had in myself, the stubbornness to reach the Top of the Everest was like a real fire which made me powerful and the goal was clear till the end.

ABOUT

Megha Parmar the first lady to conquer Mount Everest from Madhya Pradesh would be leading the Expedition. She is the most dynamic lady being from a Bhojnagar village in Sehore district of Madhya Pradesh beating all odds she achieved this success on 22 May 2019.

The only thing revolving around my head was words of my coach that it’s not difficult to climb Mt Everest, but most difficult is to descend, my Sherpa then asked for help from other Sherpa, and again due to my fortunate luck, I got an oxygen cylinder and made it to the base camp, I asked for the person who gave me his/her cylinder to show my gratitude but I got to know the cylinder belongs to late miss Anjali Kulkarni who died while climbing the mountain, may her soul rest in peace.
After that, my feelings had no limitations as I was extremely delightful and was feeling pumped up as I finally created history with my name by becoming the first woman from Madhya Pradesh to climb Mt Everest.

BACKGROUND

I come from a nuclear family, my father is a farmer in the same village and my mother is a homemaker. My village only had one government school with a ratio declined towards the boys and only offering till the fifth standard.

My biggest support was my father who held me throughout my journey before and even after my summit. He had my back every single time from my schooling and urge to study more even after declination from the people of the society he stood my me to go for higher studies, he felt the urge that I had beneath myself to study so he came with me crossing all the odds. Further, I did my studies from Acodiya Mandi District Shajapur, and being apart from the family I gained the strength and became independently strong to face every challenge coming towards me.
